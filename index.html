Nils Bohlin

The Innovator who transformed automotive safety

  • 1920 - Nils Bohlin was born on 17 july 1920, in harnosand sweden.
  • 1939 - He graduated from Harnosand Laroverk with a Bachelor of Science degree in mechanical engineering.
  • 1942 - Bohlin began his career at Saab, focusing on aircraft design and developing ejection seats for pilots.
  • 1958 - He joined Volvo as the company's first chief safety engineer, where he made his most significant contribution to automotive safety.
  • 1959 - In 1959, Bohlin developed the modern three-point seatbelt, which secured both the upper and lower body, providing superior protection compared to previous designs.
    He introduced his invention to to the Volvo company and received his first patent. Volvo introduced this innovative seatbelt design in their cars, setting a new standard for automotive safety.
  • 1961 - Volvo made the patent available to other manufacturers for free, prioritizing public safety over profit.
  • 1974 - He was awarded the Ralph Isbrandt Automotive Safety Engineering Award.
  • 1989 - He was inducted into the Hall of Fame for Safety and Health.
  • 1995 - He received a gold medal from Royal Swedish Academy of Engineering Sciences.
  • 1999 - He was inducted into the Automotive Hall of Fame.
  • 2002 - Bohlin died on 26 September 2002, at the age of 82.

The three-point seatbelt has been credited with saving over a million lives worldwide.
